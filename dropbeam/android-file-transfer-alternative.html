Google discontinued Android File Transfer. Here is what to use instead on your Mac.

Updated July 2026

Android File Transfer was never loved, but it worked. In 2024 Google quietly discontinued it, the download page went away, and on recent versions of macOS it often fails to detect the phone at all. If you are here because it stopped working, you are not alone: there is no official replacement.

What actually happened

Google retired its desktop transfer utility for Mac in mid 2024 and pointed users toward cloud services instead. Nothing first-party fills the gap. Quick Share for Windows exists, but there is no Google-made option for macOS.

Your real options in 2026

1. Cable plus a third-party MTP app. OpenMTP is free and open source and works well when the connection cooperates. Cables are fastest for a huge one-time migration, but MTP on macOS remains finicky, and every macOS update is a new adventure.

2. Cloud round trips. Upload from the phone to Google Drive or Photos, wait, download on the Mac, delete from the cloud if you care about space. It works, it is slow, your photos take a detour through a data center, and compressed exports can quietly lose quality.

3. Local Wi-Fi transfer. The phone and the Mac talk directly over your own network. Nothing round-trips through a server, full-size files arrive as recorded, and no cable is involved. This is where most people should land, and it is why I built DropBeam.
